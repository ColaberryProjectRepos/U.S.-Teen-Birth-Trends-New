Problem Statement 1

Teenage pregnancies and births have significantly decreased since data collection began, yet they remain key contributors to maternal and child mortality, which makes them the leading cause of death for girls aged 15 to 19 years globally. The United States has one of the highest rates among developed nations. In 2018, the birth rate for girls and women aged 15-19 was 41.5 births per 1,000, less than half the rate in 2008. This marks a decline of 58% and 4% for pregnancies and births respectively from 2008 to 2018. Important to note is that the reality of teenage pregnancies and births differs greatly from what the media portrays.
